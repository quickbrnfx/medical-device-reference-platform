# Stakeholder Analysis

## Overview

Stakeholder Analysis is the process of identifying the individuals, groups, and organizations that influence, interact with, develop, support, regulate, or are affected by a medical device throughout its lifecycle.

It establishes the foundation for understanding who the system must ultimately serve and whose needs must be considered during development.

---

## Purpose

Every engineering decision affects one or more stakeholders.

The purpose of Stakeholder Analysis is to identify those stakeholders early, understand their relationship to the system, and ensure that no significant perspective is overlooked during product definition.

A complete stakeholder analysis supports better user needs, more complete requirements, and improved system traceability.

---

## When It Is Performed

Stakeholder Analysis is performed during Product Definition before requirements engineering begins.

Because stakeholder needs influence all subsequent engineering activities, identifying stakeholders early reduces the risk of developing a system that fails to address important operational, technical, regulatory, or business concerns.

---

## Process

A typical stakeholder analysis consists of the following activities:

1. Define the system under consideration.
2. Identify everyone who interacts with or influences the system throughout its lifecycle.
3. Group stakeholders by their relationship to the system.
4. Document each stakeholder's role and responsibilities.
5. Determine which stakeholders directly influence product definition.
6. Use those primary stakeholders to develop personas and user needs.

---

## Characteristics of a Good Stakeholder Analysis

An effective stakeholder analysis should be:

- Comprehensive across the product lifecycle.
- Independent of implementation decisions.
- Focused on stakeholder roles rather than specific individuals.
- Traceable to downstream engineering artifacts.
- Maintainable as the product evolves.

---

## Relationship to Other Artifacts

Stakeholder Analysis is the first step in understanding the problem space.

Its outputs provide the basis for the engineering artifacts that follow.

```text
Project Charter
        │
        ▼
Stakeholder Analysis
        │
        ▼
Stakeholder Personas
        │
        ▼
User Needs
        │
        ▼
System Requirements
```

Stakeholder Analysis identifies **who** has an interest in the system.

Stakeholder Personas focus on the primary operational stakeholders.

User Needs capture the goals of those stakeholders.

System Requirements define the capabilities needed to satisfy those goals.
