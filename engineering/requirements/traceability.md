# Traceability

## Overview

Traceability is the ability to establish and maintain relationships between engineering artifacts throughout the product lifecycle.

It provides visibility into why engineering decisions were made, how system requirements were derived, and how compliance is demonstrated through verification.

Traceability connects engineering activities into a coherent and auditable development process.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of traceability is to preserve the relationships between stakeholder goals, engineering decisions, system requirements, architecture, implementation, and verification.

Maintaining these relationships improves change management, impact analysis, verification planning, and confidence that the completed system satisfies stakeholder expectations.

---

## Traceability Principles

Effective engineering traceability should be:

- Complete
- Bidirectional
- Consistent
- Maintainable
- Objective

Every engineering artifact should exist for a reason and should be connected to the artifacts that justify and validate its existence.

---

## Traceability Relationships

Although each project defines its own traceability model, a typical systems engineering workflow follows the progression below.

```text
Stakeholders
      │
      ▼
User Needs
      │
      ▼
System Requirements
      │
      ▼
Architecture
      │
      ▼
Implementation
      │
      ▼
Verification
```

This progression allows engineering teams to answer questions such as:

- Why does this requirement exist?
- Which stakeholder need does it satisfy?
- Which architectural elements implement it?
- How will it be verified?
- What is affected if it changes?

---

## Maintaining Traceability

Traceability should be established as engineering artifacts are created rather than reconstructed after development.

Maintaining traceability throughout the project supports:

- Impact analysis
- Change management
- Verification planning
- Regulatory documentation
- Engineering reviews

As systems evolve, traceability should evolve with them.

---

## Relationship to Other Artifacts

Traceability spans the entire engineering lifecycle.

Rather than existing as a standalone activity, it connects Product Definition, Requirements Engineering, Architecture, Implementation, and Verification into a single engineering narrative.

Every engineering artifact should have identifiable upstream rationale and downstream evidence.
