# Architecture Decision Record (ADR) Template

**Status:** Proposed | Accepted | Superseded | Deprecated

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD

**Decision Makers:** Project Maintainer

---

# Decision Statement

Provide a concise statement describing the engineering decision.

The Decision Statement should allow a reader to understand the outcome without reading the remainder of the document.

Example:

> The project adopts a layered software architecture for the Version 1 reference platform.

---

# Context

Describe the engineering problem, assumptions, constraints, and background that required this decision.

Answer the question:

> *Why is this decision necessary?*

If a Decision Analysis (DA) exists, reference it here rather than reproducing the detailed evaluation.

---

# Decision

State the accepted engineering decision clearly and concisely.

Focus on **what** was decided rather than **why** it was selected.

---

# Decision Principles

Identify the engineering principles that guided the decision.

Examples include:

- Simplicity
- Traceability
- Safety
- Maintainability
- Extensibility
- Security by Design
- Human-Centered Design

---

# Decision Rationale

Summarize the engineering reasoning supporting the selected decision.

Describe the key considerations that influenced the outcome without duplicating the detailed analysis contained in a supporting Decision Analysis.

---

# Alternatives Considered

Summarize the significant alternatives that were evaluated.

If a Decision Analysis exists, reference it rather than reproducing detailed comparisons.

Example:

- Layered Architecture
- Event-Driven Architecture
- Monolithic Architecture

---

# Consequences

## Positive

Describe the expected engineering benefits resulting from this decision.

Examples include:

- Improved maintainability
- Reduced complexity
- Simplified verification
- Better architectural separation

## Negative

Describe known trade-offs or limitations introduced by the decision.

---

# Future Considerations

Describe circumstances that may require this decision to be revisited.

Include anticipated future enhancements or follow-on engineering decisions where appropriate.

---

# Traceability

## Inputs

Engineering artifacts that informed this decision.

Examples:

- Project Charter
- Engineering Lifecycle
- Decision Analysis
- Requirements
- Risk Analysis

---

## Outputs

Engineering artifacts affected by this decision.

Examples:

- Architecture
- Requirements
- Verification Strategy
- Cybersecurity
- Risk Engineering

---

# References

List supporting engineering documentation.

Examples:

- Project Charter

- Requirements

- Previous ADRs
