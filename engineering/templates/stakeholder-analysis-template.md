# Stakeholder Analysis Template

## Engineering Question

Who has an interest in the system, and how do those stakeholders influence engineering decisions?

---

## Purpose

This template provides a structured approach for identifying stakeholders, understanding their interests, and documenting how those interests influence system development.

Stakeholder Analysis establishes the foundation for User Needs, System Requirements, Human Factors activities, and engineering prioritization.

---

## When to Use

Use this template during Product Definition whenever:

- A new product is initiated.
- A significant product revision is planned.
- New stakeholder groups are identified.
- Operational contexts change.

---

## Instructions

For each stakeholder:

1. Identify the stakeholder or stakeholder group.
2. Describe their role.
3. Describe their primary goals.
4. Identify how they interact with the system.
5. Document engineering considerations that arise from their involvement.

Focus on engineering implications rather than organizational structure.

| Stakeholder | Role | Goals | Interaction with System | Engineering Considerations |
|-------------|------|-------|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| Patient | Wears the device | Continuous monitoring with minimal disruption | Wears monitor during daily activities | Comfort, battery life, simple indicators, reliable sensing |

## Common Pitfalls

Avoid:

- Listing departments instead of stakeholders.
- Describing implementation details instead of stakeholder goals.
- Omitting indirect stakeholders (e.g., service personnel, administrators).
- Assuming all stakeholders have identical objectives.
- Treating the document as static rather than updating it as the project evolves.
