# Verification Reporting

## Overview

Verification Reporting is the process of evaluating, documenting, and communicating the results of completed verification activities.

A Verification Report summarizes the objective evidence collected during verification and documents whether System Requirements have been satisfactorily verified. It serves as the engineering record that demonstrates the outcome of verification activities and supports future engineering decisions.

Verification Reporting transforms collected evidence into documented engineering conclusions.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of Verification Reporting is to communicate verification results in a clear, objective, and traceable manner.

Effective Verification Reporting enables engineering teams to:

- Demonstrate requirement compliance.
- Document objective evidence.
- Record deviations and unresolved issues.
- Support engineering reviews.
- Preserve verification knowledge throughout the system lifecycle.

---

## Verification Reporting Principles

Effective Verification Reporting should be:

### Objective

Conclusions should be based solely on collected evidence and predefined acceptance criteria.

### Complete

Reports should summarize all planned verification activities and their outcomes.

### Traceable

Reported results should remain linked to the System Requirements and verification activities that produced them.

### Accurate

Verification results should faithfully represent observed system behavior without interpretation beyond the available evidence.

### Maintainable

Reports should remain part of the engineering record and support future maintenance, audits, and system evolution.

---

## Developing a Verification Report

Verification Reporting typically includes:

1. Summarize completed verification activities.
2. Reference the applicable System Requirements.
3. Document collected objective evidence.
4. Compare results against acceptance criteria.
5. Record any deviations or unresolved observations.
6. State verification conclusions.
7. Preserve supporting records and references.

The report should clearly distinguish observed evidence from engineering interpretation or recommendations.
