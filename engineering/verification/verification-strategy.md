# Verification Strategy

## Overview

A Verification Strategy defines the overall approach for demonstrating that a system satisfies its specified requirements.

Rather than describing individual verification activities, the strategy establishes how verification will be organized, planned, executed, and evaluated throughout system development.

The Verification Strategy serves as the roadmap for producing objective evidence of system compliance.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of a Verification Strategy is to establish a consistent, risk-informed approach for verifying the system.

An effective strategy enables engineering teams to:

- Plan verification activities early.
- Allocate verification responsibilities.
- Select appropriate verification methods.
- Coordinate verification across engineering disciplines.
- Ensure complete requirement coverage.
- Support efficient verification throughout the project lifecycle.

---

## Verification Strategy Principles

An effective Verification Strategy should be:

### Requirement Driven

Verification activities should be derived directly from System Requirements.

### Risk Informed

Verification effort should be proportional to the potential impact of requirement failure.

### Lifecycle Integrated

Verification planning should begin during system development rather than after implementation.

### Comprehensive

The strategy should consider all relevant verification methods, engineering disciplines, and system elements.

### Traceable

The strategy should maintain clear relationships between requirements, verification activities, and objective evidence.

---

## Developing a Verification Strategy

Developing a Verification Strategy typically includes:

1. Review System Requirements.
2. Identify verification objectives.
3. Select appropriate verification methods.
4. Define verification levels.
5. Allocate verification responsibilities.
6. Identify required resources and environments.
7. Define evidence to be collected.
8. Establish completion criteria.

The resulting strategy provides a structured framework that guides all subsequent verification activities.
